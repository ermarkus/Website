Friday Night

Folded: 8in x 12in

After completing "Wednesday Night," I felt like I had missed something, so I revisited the idea in "Friday Night."

This time, I chose to convey the atmosphere of a party by means of a double image: where one can unfocus their eye and look at the colorful lights spread across the page, or bring their eye closer and read whats written on it. I printed some posters with “Why are you here?” “Why are you having fun?” and “Why?” reading along the top of them and plenty of empty space below for handwritten responses. Then I hung the posters on my wall, put a handful of markers next to each one, and threw a party.

The questions were chosen to provoke a sincere response, and in turn gather real data about the kids attending this party. Some responses included “Parties are social, parties are political,” “F*** this,” “ “Why not?” “None of your business!” and “I don’t know.”

Expanded: 32in x 24in

After the data was collected, I took photos of the posters in disco light. I put these photographs into a fold-out that expanded in size with each new question, and lead to a large poster-within-a-poster that read "Why?"
